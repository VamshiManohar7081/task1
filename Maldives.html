Maldives

The Maldives,[d] officially the Republic of Maldives,[e] and historically known as the Maldive Islands, is a country and archipelagic state in South Asia in the Indian Ocean.[10] The Maldives is southwest of Sri Lanka and India, about 750 kilometres (470 miles; 400 nautical miles) from the Asian continent's mainland. The Maldives' chain of 26 atolls stretches across the equator from Ihavandhippolhu Atoll in the north to Addu Atoll in the south.The Maldives is a stunning tropical paradise located in the Indian Ocean, southwest of Sri Lanka and India. It’s known for its beautiful white-sand beaches, crystal-clear waters, and vibrant coral reefs. The country is made up of 26 atolls, which are home to over 1,000 islands, making it a popular destination for honeymooners, divers, and beach lovers1.

January is a great time to visit the Maldives, as it falls in the middle of the dry season, with low rainfall and humidity and clear blue skies. The average temperature during the day is around 27°C, with a high of 29°C, and at night, it drops slightly to 25°C. The sea temperature is around 28°C, making it perfect for swimming, snorkeling, and diving. The Maldives experiences around nine hours of sunshine a day in January, with just five wet days in the month, and the sun sets around 6:10 p.m. in the evening. The southern atolls of the Maldives may experience some rainfall, but it is usually rare during this time of the year.
